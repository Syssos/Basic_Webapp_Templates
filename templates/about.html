How I got to a tech school in SF:

Finding how to start something is the hardest part of any challange or task you may have to take on. Once The ball is rolling it's easier to take them on. Finding a great school for how I learn and what they offer is a big challange as there are many choice's, and its hard to tell which place will really give you everything you need to start a carrer. I met a person who went to this school called Holberton, he told me about the project based learning approche, he was working in the indestry when he talked to me about the program. He told me that the way you tackle tasks is very simular to the real world. As a student at this school you are learning code, but primarily how to teach yourself, so when you get givin a challange task it's not a new experiance and you are well versed on how to approche the problem.

What we covered in School:

The material each batch of students encounter may change as time goes on. This is vital for a tech school as the world of technology is contantly changing and in order to keep the students up to date on what's being used "now" in the indestry is important for the student's. This allows the student's to quickly be able to jump right in. The course of the program is 2 years long, however it is common for students to have job's after the first 9 months, because the material was kept up to date. The school does with the help of 150+ mentor's. These are people in the industry that help shape the corriculom to make sure that the material stays relavant to right now.
